What is Jet washing and Sealant installation ?

1. Jet Washing

Jet washing (or pressure washing) is the process of cleaning surfaces using high-pressure water. This step removes dirt, grime, moss, algae, and other debris that can accumulate on outdoor surfaces.

Preparation:
  • Clear the Area: Remove any furniture, plants, or other items from the area you intend to clean.
  • Safety Gear: Wear protective clothing, including safety glasses, gloves, and waterproof boots.
Jet Washing:
  • Connect the Pressure Washer: Attach your pressure washer to a water source and ensure it’s set up correctly.
  • Choose the Right Nozzle: Use a nozzle that’s appropriate for the surface. For example, a 25-degree nozzle for general cleaning.
  • Start Cleaning: Begin jet washing, keeping the nozzle at a consistent distance from the surface (usually around 12-18 inches) to avoid damage.
  • Rinse Thoroughly: Make sure to rinse the entire area thoroughly to remove any remaining dirt or cleaning agents.

2. Sealant Installation

After the surface is thoroughly cleaned and dried, applying a sealant helps protect it from future stains, water damage, and wear. Sealants can enhance the appearance of the surface by bringing out its natural color and providing a glossy finish.

Selecting the Sealant:
  • Type of Surface: Choose a sealant that is appropriate for the material of the surface (e.g., concrete, stone, wood).
  • Finish Preference: Decide whether you want a matte, semi-gloss, or high-gloss finish.
Application:
  • Preparation: Ensure the surface is completely dry and free of any debris.
  • Apply the Sealant: Use a roller or brush for even application. For larger areas, a sprayer may be more efficient.
  • Multiple Coats: Depending on the product, you might need to apply multiple coats. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for drying times between coats.
  • Regular Cleaning: Keep the sealed surface clean by regularly sweeping and washing it.
